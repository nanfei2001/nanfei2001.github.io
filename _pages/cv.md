Profile
======
I am a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at UC Santa Barbara, advised by Prof. Mahnoosh Alizadeh. My work focuses on optimization, game theory, machine learning, and market design.

My current research develops theoretically grounded algorithms for online decision-making under safety constraints, sampling limits, and incentive constraints, with applications in stochastic gradient methods, safe learning-based control, and EV charging markets.

Research Experience
======
**Machine Learning Theory and Optimization Algorithms**

UC Santa Barbara, September 2023-present

Developed strategic-querying variants of stochastic gradient methods to improve transient learning performance and sampling efficiency. Analyzed convergence behavior and algorithmic tradeoffs through mathematical modeling and literature synthesis.

**Market Design, Pricing, and Game-Theoretic Modeling**

UC Santa Barbara, June 2024-present

Studied pricing and incentive design in capacity-constrained markets, using convex duality and game-theoretic modeling to analyze strategic behavior, allocation efficiency, and operator decisions. Applications include EV charging access and other discrete resource markets.

**Safe Learning-Based Control**

UC Santa Barbara, October 2023-present

Worked on learning-based control under safety constraints for autonomous and engineering systems. Developed algorithms for constrained linear-quadratic control and online nonstochastic control, emphasizing safety, robustness, and performance guarantees.

**Machine Learning in Optimal Control**

UC Santa Cruz, May 2022-August 2022

Supervisor: Prof. Qi Gong

Worked on data-driven machine learning for optimal control, including structured physics-informed neural networks for fitting nonconvex control functions.
