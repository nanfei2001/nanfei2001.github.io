About the Show
==============

The Next Elevator is not trying to predict the future correctly. It is meant to preserve some room for sincere expression and speculative thinking. We do not pretend to be the most authoritative voices in the room, and we may not reach a standard answer. The aim is simply to take a few large questions about AI seriously while keeping the conversation open, curious, and relaxed.

Across conversations with guests from different fields, I hope to collect distinctive observations, genuine uncertainty, and unusual ideas—and to ask how ordinary people might imagine a world after AI beyond the usual technical and commercial narratives.

Why “The Next Elevator”?
========================

The name is inspired by the “elevator paradox.” Nineteenth-century visions of future skyscrapers anticipated restaurants, barbershops, and stores inside the buildings. Some even imagined that, because moving between floors would be so inconvenient, each level might gradually become a closed society of its own. Science-fiction writers built many possible futures on that premise.

What they missed was the elevator. Once it arrived, every forecast based on the assumption that taller buildings must make daily life less convenient was overturned. One overlooked technical variable made an entire family of imagined futures inaccurate.

AI may play a similar role in society today—and AI itself may still be reshaped by variables we have not yet seen. That is why I am skeptical of confident forecasts, but interested in thinking that remains original, unfixed, and willing to admit uncertainty.
